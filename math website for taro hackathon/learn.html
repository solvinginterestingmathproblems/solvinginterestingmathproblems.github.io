Introduction

The best way to learn is to try lots of problems, by yourself. Each problem comes with hints that you can click on when you get stuck.

General Tips

  1. Read the problem carefully!
  2. If it is multiple choice, look at the answer options: do you need to round? Will you keep it in terms of pi?
  3. Check to make sure you are answering the problem asked. You may have solved for a variable, but they might ask you for the sum of your variables instead.
  4. It can help to write algebraic equations.
  5. But don't go overboard with variables!
  6. If it is a geometry problem, try to draw a diagram.
  7. Label everything you know, but don't make it too messy.
  8. When reading geometry problems, look for things such as parallel lines, similar triangles, special triangles, right angles, etc.
