Problem #1, Solution

Source: 2017 AMC 8, #25

We begin by "adding" our circle sixths to our drawing. In our diagram, the points X and Y are each centers of these imaginary circles.

We note that angle SXR and angle TYR each measure 60 degrees because they are one-sixth of a circle's 360 degrees. We recall that we were given that angle TUS measured 60 degrees. Because all three angles in our triangle are 60 degrees, we know that triangle XUY is an equilateral triangle.

We were told that the circle sectors were those of a circle with radius 2, and also that segments US and UT each also measured 2. Thus our equilateral triangle XUY has side lengths of 2 + 2 = 4.

Now we can see that to calculate the area of the region SUTR, we simply need to subtract the area of the two circle sectors of radius 2 from the area of an equilateral triangle of side length 4. These two are common shapes with basic formulas for finding their area. (At this time, the calculations will not be performed here.)

Thus, our final answer is B.
